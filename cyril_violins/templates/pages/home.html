{% trans "About" %}

I am an experienced luthier having spent many years under master craftsmen of Asia honing my skills in the fine art of violin making.

My obsession with violin-making began in my youth when I first heard Paganini's Caprice No. 24 performed on an 18th-century Stradivari. The instrument's voice—rich, complex, and hauntingly beautiful—captured my imagination.

I source my materials meticulously—maple from the Balkans that has been naturally aged for at least 15 years, spruce from the Italian Alps, the same forest where Stradivari himself obtained his wood. Each piece is selected not just for its grain pattern or density, but for its acoustic properties.

In an age of mass production and quick turnarounds, I strive for perfection in my instruments and aim to provide my customers with affordable services.
